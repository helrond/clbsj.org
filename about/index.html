The Center and Library for the Bible and Social Justice is a media and organizing hub that connects biblical scholarship with our movements for social justice. We strive to connect biblically informed scholars, students, clergy, activists, artists and community organizers who often work separately and without the rich resources and insights they are capable of offering one another.

We do this by providing resources — including books, periodicals, archival and electronic data — and by currating online educational programming on topics and problems central to furthing the critical study of the Bible and to empowering engagement with the Bible in our justice work today. We aim to create a synergy of theory and practice in a dynamic action-reflection climate that will make these ideas exciting and accessible to specialist and lay audiences alike.
